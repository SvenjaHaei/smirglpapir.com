Chester

The tabletop has been constructed from two wooden oak planks to allow for a rustic bark look on both sides and at the same time having enough width for the chesstable (Bauhaus). For a good grip the planks have been fixed together with wooden dowels and wood glue. To cover the intersecting line I used chipped wood from the original planks and sprinkled it into the wood glue. The chess field has been burned inside the wood by using a butane torch covering the white pieces with tiles. The legs are black, steel hairpin table legs (Amazon).

The figures are made from cement. I build a little cubic form to fill in the cement and added details depending on the figure type. White is natural cement color. For dark I added some black color pigment to the cement:

  • Queen: mini lightbulb
  • King: gold flanged hex head bolt
  • Pawn: small cube
  • Rook: big cube
  • Knight: 7/8 cube
  • Bishop: rounded sides
